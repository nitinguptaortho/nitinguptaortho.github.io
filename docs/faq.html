Frequently Ask Question

FAQs

1. What does an orthopedic surgeon specialize in?

- An orthopedic surgeon, also known as a joint specialist, specializes in the diagnosis, treatment, prevention, and rehabilitation of disorders, injuries, and diseases of the musculoskeletal system.

2. How do I know if I need knee replacement surgery?

- If you experience chronic knee pain, swelling, or stiffness that limits daily activities and doesn't improve with medications, consulting with the best knee replacement surgeon in Jaipur would be advised.

3. What is the difference between a ligament specialist and a joint specialist?

- While both deal with the musculoskeletal system, a ligament specialist focuses specifically on ligament injuries and conditions, whereas a joint specialist broadly addresses issues related to joints, including arthritis and joint replacements.

4. How long does it take to recover from hip joint replacement surgery?

- Recovery time can vary, but most patients resume normal activities within 6-12 weeks. It's essential to follow post-surgery guidelines provided by the joint replacement surgeon in Jaipur for optimal recovery.

5. Are there non-surgical treatments available for knee pain?

- Yes, there are several non-surgical treatments available. Consulting a knee specialist in Jaipur can provide insights into options like physical therapy, medications, and injections.

6. How do I choose the best bone doctor in Jaipur for my condition?

- Consider factors like experience, patient reviews, and qualifications. It's also beneficial to seek referrals and meet the doctor for an initial consultation.

7. Is joint replacement surgery safe?

- When performed by a qualified joint replacement surgeon in Jaipur, these surgeries have a high success rate and are generally safe. However, as with any surgery, there are risks involved.

8. What conditions can lead to the need for shoulder or hip joint replacements?

- Conditions like osteoarthritis, rheumatoid arthritis, post-traumatic arthritis, rotator cuff tear arthropathy, and avascular necrosis can lead to the need for joint replacements.

9. When should I see a ligament specialist?

- If you've had a sports injury, accident, or experienced persistent pain, swelling, or instability in a joint, seeing a ligament specialist is advisable.

10. What are the common treatments provided by an orthopaedic surgeon?

- An orthopaedic surgeon provides a range of treatments from diagnostic services, non-surgical interventions, to surgeries like knee, hip, and shoulder replacements.
